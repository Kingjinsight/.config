# Drift — Surface Unconscious Patterns

Find what ideas King keeps returning to across unrelated notes, without intending to.

## Process

1. **Collect all notes** across `~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Knowledge/`, `~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Daily Notes/`, `~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Reviews/`, `Inbox/`

2. **Extract recurring phrases and concepts**
   - Look for words/phrases appearing 3+ times across different folders
   - Ignore expected repetitions (project names, place names, task words)
   - Focus on conceptual words: themes, values, framings, metaphors

3. **Group by semantic cluster** — related terms that point to the same underlying interest
   - e.g., "autonomy", "self-directed", "own path" → one cluster

4. **Find the unintentional ones** — themes that appear in:
   - Multiple unrelated folders (not just one project)
   - Notes written weeks or months apart
   - Places where they weren't the main topic

5. **Rate by surprise** — how unexpected is this given King's stated interests?

## Output Format

---

**Drift Report — [date]**

**You keep coming back to...**

**[Theme 1]**
Appears in: [[note]], [[note]], [[note]] ([date range])
> "[representative quote from one of the notes]"
*Signal strength: [how often / how spread out]*

**[Theme 2]**
Appears in: [[note]], [[note]]
> "[quote]"
*Signal strength: ...*

**The most unexpected drift**
[Theme King probably doesn't think of as a core interest, but the vault says otherwise]

**What this might mean**
[1-2 sentences: what does this pattern suggest about what King actually cares about vs what he says he cares about?]

---

## Rules

- Drift is about the subconscious — prioritize surprising over obvious
- Pull actual quotes — the specific language matters as much as the topic
- Don't include themes that are explicit in ~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Now.md or ~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Goals.md — those are intentional
- The "most unexpected drift" is the most valuable output
