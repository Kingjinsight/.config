# Challenge — Pressure-Test Beliefs

Find contradictions, weak assumptions, and blind spots in King's thinking on a topic.

## Input

`/challenge [topic]`
If no topic, ask: "What belief or idea should I challenge?"

## Process

1. **Gather all relevant notes** on the topic across the vault

2. **Look for contradictions**
   - Did King say opposite things at different times?
   - Does his stated belief conflict with his logged actions (daily notes)?
   - Does his `~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Values.md` conflict with `~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/~/Documents/Obsidian/personalOS/Anti-Goals.md` on this topic?

3. **Identify assumptions**
   - What does King take for granted that isn't stated?
   - What would have to be true for his position to hold?
   - What evidence is he NOT citing?

4. **Find weak points**
   - Where is the reasoning thin or circular?
   - What's the strongest counterargument he hasn't addressed?
   - What would someone who disagrees say?

5. **Check against reality** (from daily notes)
   - Does he actually live what he says he believes?
   - What does his behavior reveal that his stated beliefs don't?

## Output Format

---

**Challenging: [topic]**

**What you've said**
[Brief summary of King's stated position, with note references]

**Contradictions found**
- [contradiction 1] — [[note A]] vs [[note B]]
- [contradiction 2] — stated belief vs logged behavior

**Assumptions worth questioning**
- "[assumption]" — [why it might be wrong]

**The strongest counterargument**
[Most compelling challenge to his position]

**What your behavior reveals**
[What daily notes show vs what he claims to believe]

**Question to sit with**
[One sharp question that cuts to the core tension]

---

## Rules

- Be genuinely critical — not performatively challenging, actually looking for weak points
- Ground every challenge in specific notes, not generic arguments
- The "behavior vs belief" gap is often the most revealing — prioritize it
- End with one question, not a list — the sharpest one
